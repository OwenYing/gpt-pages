About BMI (Body Mass Index)

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple calculation that uses your height and weight to estimate body fat and assess whether you're at a healthy weight for your height. While BMI is a useful screening tool, it's important to remember that it doesn't directly measure body fat or account for factors like muscle mass, bone density, and overall body composition.

What BMI Tells Us

  • Underweight: BMI below 18.5 - may indicate nutritional deficiencies
  • Normal Weight: BMI 18.5-24.9 - generally considered healthy range
  • Overweight: BMI 25.0-29.9 - may increase health risks
  • Obese: BMI 30.0 and above - significantly increased health risks

Understanding Your BMI Results

For Underweight (BMI < 18.5)

  • May indicate nutritional deficiencies
  • Could affect immune system function
  • May lead to osteoporosis and other health issues
  • Consider consulting a healthcare provider about healthy weight gain

For Normal Weight (BMI 18.5-24.9)

  • Generally associated with good health
  • Lower risk of weight-related health problems
  • Maintain with balanced diet and regular exercise
  • Focus on overall fitness and healthy lifestyle habits

For Overweight (BMI 25.0-29.9)

  • May increase risk of health problems
  • Higher risk of heart disease, diabetes, and other conditions
  • Consider lifestyle changes: diet and exercise
  • Consult healthcare provider for personalized advice

For Obese (BMI ≥ 30.0)

  • Significantly increased health risks
  • Higher risk of serious health conditions
  • May require medical intervention and support
  • Important to work with healthcare professionals

Important Disclaimer

This BMI calculator is for informational purposes only. BMI is a screening tool and does not diagnose health conditions. It doesn't account for muscle mass, bone density, age, sex, ethnicity, or overall body composition. Always consult with a healthcare professional for personalized health advice and to discuss any concerns about your weight or health.
